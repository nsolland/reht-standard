This policy governs use of the names, marks and compatibility claims associated with reht.

The Apache License 2.0 applies to code, schemas, examples and repository automation. It does not grant trademark rights.

## 2. Permitted descriptive use

You may use the word `reht` only for accurate descriptive reference, including:

- stating that software implements or evaluates against the public reht specification;
- linking to this repository;
- describing compatibility testing performed against a specific published version;
- identifying modifications derived from this repository, provided the modified work is not presented as official.

Descriptive use must not imply sponsorship, certification, endorsement, partnership or approval by VALO Research Group AS.

## 3. Restricted terms

The following claims may not be used without written authorization from VALO Research Group AS:

- `official reht`
- `reht certified`
- `certified reht`
- `reht approved`
- `reht verified`
- `VALO approved`
- any confusingly similar certification or endorsement claim

## 4. Compatibility terminology

### `reht-compatible`

May be used only when an implementation:

1. identifies the exact reht specification version used;
2. passes all mandatory schema and conformance tests for the claimed profile;
3. publishes the test method and test date;
4. does not imply certification or endorsement;
5. clearly identifies any extensions, deviations or unsupported requirements.

Recommended form:

> Compatible with reht Standard v0.1.0-draft at the schema level. Not certified or endorsed by VALO Research Group AS.

### `reht-conformant`

May be used only when an implementation satisfies every mandatory requirement in the claimed conformance level and publishes a signed conformance statement.

Recommended form:

> Self-declared conformant with reht Standard v0.1.0-draft, Level [X]. This is not an official certification.

### `reht-certified`

Reserved exclusively for a certification programme formally operated or authorized by VALO Research Group AS. No self-certification claim may use this term.

## 5. Forks and derivative standards

Forks and derivative works are permitted under the applicable repository licence, but they must:

- use a distinct project and product name;
- preserve required copyright, licence and attribution notices;
- identify material changes;
- avoid using reht logos or visual identity;
- avoid presenting the derivative as the official reht standard;
- state clearly that the work is independent and not endorsed by VALO Research Group AS.

No licence is granted to use reht or VALO logos, icons, badges, certification marks or visual identity assets except with written permission.
